My name is Jainil, and I'm a junior at UC Berkeley majoring in computer science.

As a child, I would often find myself solving riddles and playing the classic Windows 7 sliding puzzle. Although I parted ways with my antiquated PC in 2013, my love for puzzles remained intact. I sought programming as an outlet for my problem-solving instinct, relishing the challenge of solving algorithmic "puzzles."

On this website, I've attempted to catalogue some of the puzzles I've tinkered with and experience I've accrued.

Last summer, I interned at Salesforce and worked on Salesforce's data integrations platform, MuleSoft Composer. Presently, I'm exploring problems in robot grasping and manipulation at BAIR.

In my free time, I enjoy playing cricket, hitting the climbing gym with my friends, and geeking out over sports.

I'm also particularly fond of making jokes, much to my friends' dismay delight. Pathetic puns, elite multi-layered jokes (database lovers will back me up here), one-liners-that-probably-shouldn't-exist, you got it all.
