About Me!

Hi there! My name is Jainil Ajmera. I am 15 years old, and I currently study at Delhi Public School, Gurgaon. I was born on November 21, 2001 in Andheri, a bustling suburb of Mumbai. I enjoy problem-solving and have an array of diverse interests, including robotics, programming, web development, filmmaking and playing the guitar.

I have several awards (both national and international) to my credit. Some of the more prestigious of these include being ranked 18th internationally in the National Cyber Olympiad (which is now conducted in several countries across Asia) and being a part of a First Tech Challenge (FTC) team that bagged numerous awards, such as the Inspire Award and PTC Design Award, at regional events.

Being a member of the Metal Marauders, a private FTC team based in New Jersey, remains one of my most cherished experiences. By exposing me to the various aspects of robotics, FTC helped deepen my passion for the STEM field and turned me into a better problem-solver. In addition, the program helped me learn how to share my ideas with others by providing me with the opportunity to become involved with the community. I am extremely thankful to the other members of the team, who shared their knowledge with me and supported me throughout the season.

Building on these experiences, I participated in another competition called World Robot Olympiad (WRO) last year. Our team, High Voltage, secured the second position in the Regular Junior High category of WRO 2016.

FTC 2015

FIRST Tech Challenge is a robotics competition designed for students in grades 7-12. Teams are responsible for designing, building, and programming their robots in a 10-week build period to compete in an alliance format against other teams. Teams are also required to make an 'Engineering Notebook' that documents their build season and experience. Res-Q, the 2015-16 edition of the First Tech Challenge, challenged students to design and build robots capable of climbing ramps, depositing debris in designated areas and hanging, among other tasks. Our robot featured a robust drivetrain, a 3D-printed bucket for efficient collection of debris, as well as a cascading slide system capable of extending to maximum length in less than 4 seconds.

WRO 2016

World Robot Olympiad is a global robotics competition that brings together enthusiastic students from all across the world. The 2016 season, Rap the Scrap, was held in Delhi, India in November 2016. The Regular Junior High challenge required a robot to autonomously identify and locate different types of wastes (denoted by cubes of different sizes and colours) found inside the house, and take them to their respective bins, to sort the waste. The challenge required locating the waste bins (also differentiated by colors, and location), identifying different wastes available, and matching the wastes to their respective bins using an algorithm. Finally, to score points, the robot needed to pick the correct waste, deposit them in the bins, and return to the start area.

FTC 2016

Velocity Vortex, the 2016-17 edition of the FIRST Tech Challenge, required a robot to perform a variety of different tasks. The match began with a 30 second autonomous period, where the robots could only operate by pre-programmed instructions. In this period, points were awarded if the robot pressed the correct beacon buttons, successfully shot 'particles' (balls) into designated areas and/or parked on a ramp or raised platform. The autonomous period was followed by a 2 minute driver-controlled period, where points were awarded if the robot shot particles into the designated areas and/or lifted a 'cap ball' (gym ball).
